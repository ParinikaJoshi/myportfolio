About Me.

I'm Parinika Joshi, a licensed dental surgeon turned clinical data scientist — and that's not a detour, it's my edge. My clinical background means I don't just model data; I understand what it represents at the bedside and how a clinician actually uses evidence to make decisions.

I hold an MPH in Epidemiology & Biostatistics from Texas A&M School of Public Health (GPA 4.0). I specialize in large-scale observational data, real-world evidence generation, clinical ontology mapping, and causal inference. I've built ETL pipelines for 100,000+ record longitudinal studies, mapped clinical datasets to the OMOP Common Data Model, and published on tobacco cessation, diabetes disparities, and arsenic-related cardiometabolic outcomes.

I work at the intersection of clinical knowledge, health data infrastructure, and generative AI — converting complex clinical questions into structured, evidence-backed insights. I use LLMs daily as force multipliers: for clinical phenotyping, ontology mapping, pipeline development, and content generation. My goal is to help close the evidence gap between data and the decisions that improve patient lives.
